1. Who controls information

Xeronin operates the covered Services and determines how information needed to provide and secure those Services is processed. In some server-specific situations, a Discord server owner or administrator also determines why a bot feature is enabled and how resulting information is used within that server.

Discord independently controls information it processes to operate the Discord platform. This Policy does not replace Discord’s Privacy Policy.

2. Information processed

The exact information processed depends on the bot, command, feature, and server configuration. The Services may process the following categories:

  • Discord identifiers: user IDs, server IDs, channel IDs, role IDs, message IDs, application-command IDs, and similar platform identifiers.
  • Profile and server information: usernames, display names, avatars, server names, role information, permissions, membership information, and other data Discord makes available to the bot for an enabled feature.
  • Commands and interactions: command names, options, button or menu selections, timestamps, responses, and related interaction metadata.
  • Configuration data: server preferences, enabled modules, selected channels or roles, permission settings, and feature-specific settings.
  • Content supplied to a feature: text, attachments, links, or other material intentionally submitted through a command, form, ticket, moderation workflow, or other bot interaction.
  • Moderation, safety, and abuse data: warnings, actions, reports, rule matches, audit information, or evidence when a server enables a relevant feature.
  • Technical and security data: timestamps, error reports, diagnostic information, request outcomes, rate-limit information, and limited logs used to operate, troubleshoot, and protect the Services.
  • Support communications: information you provide when requesting help, reporting abuse, appealing an action, or making a privacy request.

The Services do not require your Discord password or user token. Never provide those credentials to a bot or support representative.

3. Sources of information

Information may come directly from you, from Discord through its APIs and interaction systems, from a server owner or administrator who configures a feature, from other users who interact with a shared server feature, or from infrastructure providers that generate operational and security logs.

4. How information is used

Xeronin may process information to:

  • receive and respond to commands and interactions;
  • provide requested bot functions and remember server configuration;
  • apply permissions, automation, moderation, safety, or administrative settings selected by authorized server administrators;
  • authenticate requests and prevent unauthorized use;
  • maintain reliability, diagnose errors, monitor performance, and improve features;
  • detect spam, abuse, fraud, attacks, policy violations, or security incidents;
  • respond to support, safety, legal, or privacy requests;
  • comply with applicable law, court orders, and Discord requirements; and
  • establish, exercise, or defend legal rights.

Xeronin does not sell Discord user data. The Services are not designed to create advertising profiles or serve behaviorally targeted advertising.

5. Legal bases for processing

Where privacy law requires a legal basis, processing may rely on one or more of the following: performing a service you requested; Xeronin’s legitimate interests in operating, securing, supporting, and improving the Services; the legitimate interests of an authorized server administrator in configuring a requested server feature; compliance with legal obligations; protection of vital interests; or consent where consent is required.

You may withdraw consent for future processing where consent is the basis, but withdrawal does not affect processing that was lawful before withdrawal.

6. Message and interaction content

The Services may access message or interaction content only when Discord supplies it and when it is needed for an enabled command or feature. Examples may include responding to a command, evaluating content submitted to a moderation feature, processing a ticket, or generating a requested output.

Content may be processed temporarily in memory while a request is handled. It may be stored when storage is necessary for a feature chosen by a user or authorized server administrator, such as configuration, reminders, tickets, saved content, moderation records, or audit history. Xeronin seeks to limit stored content to what is reasonably necessary for the feature and its security.

Do not use the Services to submit highly sensitive information unless a feature clearly requires it and you are authorized to provide it.

7. How information is shared

Information may be disclosed only as reasonably necessary in the following circumstances:

  • Discord: interactions and responses are transmitted through Discord as necessary to operate the Services.
  • Infrastructure providers: hosting, networking, storage, database, logging, monitoring, or security providers may process limited information on Xeronin’s behalf.
  • Server participants and administrators: a command response, moderation action, log entry, ticket, or configured output may be visible to people with access to the relevant Discord channel, server, or feature.
  • Legal and safety disclosures: information may be disclosed when reasonably believed necessary to comply with law or legal process; protect a person from harm; investigate abuse, fraud, or security incidents; enforce agreements; or protect rights and property.
  • Business changes: information may be transferred as part of a merger, acquisition, financing, reorganization, sale of assets, or transfer of the Services, subject to appropriate confidentiality and legal protections.

Xeronin does not authorize service providers to use Discord data for their own advertising purposes.

8. Retention and deletion

Information is retained only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was processed, including providing an enabled feature, maintaining security and reliability, resolving disputes, enforcing agreements, and complying with law.

Retention varies by data type and feature. Server configuration may remain while a bot is installed or until an administrator resets or deletes it. Operational and security logs are generally kept for a limited period. Feature records such as reminders, tickets, moderation history, saved content, or audit entries may remain until their purpose is complete, an authorized user deletes them, the server removes the bot, or a valid deletion request is completed. Backup copies may remain until routine backup rotation completes.

Removing a bot from a server stops new server interactions but may not immediately erase all existing records. Data may be retained longer when reasonably necessary for security, fraud prevention, legal compliance, dispute resolution, or protection of another person’s rights.

9. Your choices and privacy rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or objection; to withdraw consent; and to complain to a privacy regulator. These rights may be subject to legal exceptions and identity verification.

You can also reduce processing by not using optional commands, asking a server administrator to change or disable a feature, removing your own submitted content where Discord permits, leaving a server, or asking an authorized administrator to remove the bot.

To make a request, use the official support or contact method displayed in the relevant bot’s Discord profile, application-directory page, documentation, or official server. Include the bot name, your Discord user ID, the relevant server ID if applicable, the type of request, and enough detail to locate the relevant records. Do not publish private information in a public channel.

Xeronin may need to verify control of the relevant Discord account or authority over a server before acting. Some server-controlled records may need to be handled by that server’s owner or administrators.

10. Security

Xeronin uses reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards intended to protect information, such as access restrictions, permission controls, secret management, encrypted network connections, software updates, monitoring, and incident-response practices. No service can guarantee absolute security.

If you believe the Services have a security issue, report it privately through an official support method and do not publicly expose credentials, personal information, or exploit details that could place others at risk.

11. International processing

The Services and their providers may process information in countries other than the country where you live. Those countries may have different data-protection laws. Where required, Xeronin relies on appropriate legal mechanisms and safeguards for international transfers.

12. Children

The Services are not directed to children under 13 or anyone below the minimum age required to use Discord or online services in their country. Xeronin does not knowingly collect personal information from a child who is not legally permitted to use the Services. A parent or guardian who believes such information has been provided should submit a deletion request through an official support method.

13. These legal pages

This legal site does not intentionally use advertising cookies, analytics scripts, account systems, or behavioral tracking. It is designed as a static website. The hosting and network providers used to deliver the pages may process ordinary technical information such as IP addresses, browser details, and request logs under their own privacy practices.

14. Changes to this Policy

Xeronin may update this Policy when the Services, data practices, laws, risks, providers, or Discord requirements change. The “Last updated” date will be revised when a new version is published. Material changes may also be announced through the Services or an official support channel when reasonably practical.

15. Contact and requests

Privacy questions, data requests, security reports, and legal notices may be submitted through the official support or contact method displayed in the Discord profile, application-directory page, documentation, or official server for Omniframe Bot or Steward 13 Bot.

For a privacy request, include:

  • the applicable bot name;
  • your Discord user ID;
  • the relevant server ID, if applicable;
  • whether you are requesting access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or objection; and
  • a brief description of the relevant interaction or record.
